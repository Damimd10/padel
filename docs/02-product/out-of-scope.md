# Out of Scope

## Purpose

This document protects the MVP from drifting back into the original all-in-one concept.

## Explicitly out of scope for MVP

### Full booking replacement

- Court inventory management as a full system of record.
- Full reservation engine for all venue operations.
- Broad calendar replacement across all club activities.

Why out of scope:

- The MVP is competition-first, not booking-first.

### Payments and business administration

- Membership billing.
- Tournament payments and wallets.
- Invoicing.
- Accounting flows.
- Payment reconciliation.

Why out of scope:

- These are deep operational systems with compliance and support implications unrelated to the initial competition wedge.

### General club operations

- Daily social-play coordination.
- Open-slot social match administration.
- Staff-wide operational management for all club activities.
- Full member CRM.

Why out of scope:

- These are club-operations products, not core competition workflow.

### Advanced federation-grade competition features

- Official federation sanctioning workflows.
- Deep regulatory compliance for official circuits.
- Highly customized multi-stage tournament logic across many sport-specific exceptions.

Why out of scope:

- The MVP should support amateur competition administration, not the most complex professional or federation-grade edge cases.

### Advanced ranking and analytics

- Cross-club universal rating portability.
- Deep player performance dashboards.
- Rich development analytics.
- Predictive or scouting-style statistics.

Why out of scope:

- These features depend on mature, trusted data and are not required to validate the competition administration wedge.

### Full communication replacement

- Rich chat platform behavior.
- Community-wide messaging as a standalone product.
- Real-time messaging intended to replace WhatsApp completely.

Why out of scope:

- The product should structure competition workflows, not become a chat product.

### Secondary business lines

- Coach and academy management.
- Sponsorship and merchandising flows.
- Broad reporting suites for every club function.

Why out of scope:

- These are adjacent businesses, not part of the first validated workflow.

## Scope guardrail

If a feature does not directly improve competition setup, registration, eligibility control, match progression, result handling, or standings visibility, it should be excluded from MVP unless new research proves otherwise.
