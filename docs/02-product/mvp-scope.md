# MVP Scope

## MVP objective

Validate whether clubs and organizers will adopt a structured workflow for tournament and amateur competition management, and whether that workflow reduces manual administrative effort enough to justify continued usage and commercial interest.

## MVP wedge

The MVP wedge is:

- competition workflow management for padel clubs and amateur organizers.

This means the MVP focuses on operational moments current tools often handle badly:

- tournament creation,
- category and sex segmentation,
- player registration,
- participant approval,
- fixture or bracket generation,
- result capture,
- and competition progression visibility.

## Target customer for MVP

The MVP is for:

- club managers,
- tournament organizers,
- league administrators,
- and competition-support staff

who currently run tournaments or amateur competitions using a mix of spreadsheets, chat, and generic club software.

## Core workflow to validate

The MVP should support this end-to-end flow:

1. An organizer creates a tournament or competition.
2. The organizer defines categories, sex segmentation, format, and registration rules.
3. Players register and the organizer validates entries.
4. The system creates the competition structure for approved participants.
5. Matches progress through result entry and validation.
6. The competition closes with final standings or winners clearly recorded.

## In-scope capabilities

### Competition setup

- Create tournaments or simple amateur competitions.
- Define title, dates, format, operational owner, and status.
- Support categories and sex-based segmentation.

### Participant administration

- Register players or teams.
- Review and approve entries.
- Prevent invalid participation according to competition rules.
- Maintain basic participant status visibility.

### Category and division management

- Create and manage competition categories.
- Associate participants with categories and sex-based divisions.
- Keep categories operationally separate for progression and reporting.

### Match structure management

- Generate or manage a lightweight bracket, draw, or competition match structure.
- Track match states and competition progression.
- Surface unresolved operational gaps such as missing results.

### Result handling

- Capture match results.
- Support lightweight result validation by authorized users.
- Update competition progression and standings accordingly.

### Operational visibility

- Show registration counts, approval state, active matches, pending results, and competition status.
- Give organizers and operators a practical view of what needs attention.

### Lightweight participant history

- Store basic participation and result history tied to completed competitions.

## What the MVP must prove

- Organizers use the workflow for real competitions.
- Competition setup and administration require less manual effort than current processes.
- Players can register and follow progression with acceptable clarity.
- Clubs or organizers perceive enough operational value to continue using the product.

## Scope discipline

The MVP should be considered successful if it solves one repeated competition workflow well. It does not need to look like a complete club platform.
