# Success Metrics

## Purpose

These metrics are designed to validate whether the MVP solves a real competition administration problem and creates measurable buyer value.

## Buyer-value metrics

- Reduction in manual administration time per competition.
- Reduction in competition setup time from creation to ready-to-run state.
- Reduction in organizer effort required to manage registrations, categories, and results.
- Improvement in competition completion reliability without spreadsheet rework.

## Adoption metrics

- Weekly or monthly active organizers running competitions inside the product.
- Percentage of competitions managed fully inside the product.
- Player registration completion rate.
- Entry approval completion rate before competition start.

## Operational quality metrics

- Percentage of competitions with complete participant structure before start.
- Percentage of matches with recorded results inside the product.
- Median time from played match to recorded result.
- Percentage of competitions closed without unresolved result disputes.

## Commercial validation metrics

- Time-to-first-competition after onboarding.
- Pilot-to-paid conversion rate.
- 60-day and 90-day organizer retention.
- 60-day and 90-day club retention.

## Guardrail metrics

- Manual overrides required per competition.
- Support requests related to category assignment, eligibility, or progression confusion.
- Churn reasons tied to workflow mismatch or missing competition depth.
- Player drop-off between registration and active competition participation.

## Success threshold guidance

The MVP should be considered directionally promising if it can show:

- repeated real competition usage by organizers,
- clear reduction in manual tournament administration,
- reliable result and progression handling,
- and evidence that operators would continue using or paying for the workflow.

If those signals do not appear, adding adjacent club-management features is not the right response. The competition wedge itself should be re-evaluated.
