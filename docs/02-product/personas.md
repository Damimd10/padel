# Personas

## Persona 1: Club Competition Manager

### Profile

- Responsible for organizing and overseeing competitive activity at a club.
- Often coordinates tournaments, categories, registrations, and operational staff.

### Goals

- Run competitions cleanly and predictably.
- Reduce spreadsheet and chat dependency.
- Keep category and participant administration under control.
- Maintain visibility into registrations, draws, pending results, and unresolved administrative issues.

### Frustrations

- Competition setup is manual and repetitive.
- Category and eligibility mistakes create social friction.
- Results and progression are hard to trust when handled informally.
- Staff lose time reconciling different versions of the same competition data.

### Buying perspective

- Likely buyer or strong buying influencer.
- Will reject the product if it is too generic or if it requires heavy customization for ordinary amateur competitions.

## Persona 2: Tournament Organizer

### Profile

- Runs recurring tournaments, leagues, or club events.
- Owns the day-to-day administrative workload of registrations, match flow, and results.

### Goals

- Set up competitions quickly.
- Administer categories and sex divisions clearly.
- Keep draws and match progression structured.
- Close tournaments without spreadsheet debt.

### Frustrations

- Registration data arrives inconsistently.
- Players question categories, eligibility, and progression.
- Results arrive late or in conflicting formats.
- Manual bracket and standings updates create avoidable mistakes.

### Adoption perspective

- Strong early champion.
- More likely to adopt if the product removes real operational pain immediately.

## Persona 3: Competition Support Staff

### Profile

- Helps run check-in, participant updates, same-day issue handling, and result recording during competition operations.

### Goals

- Resolve administrative issues quickly.
- See the current state of competitions and matches at a glance.
- Avoid searching across chats and spreadsheets for the latest data.

### Frustrations

- Results and participant changes are scattered.
- Same-day issues create confusion fast.
- Small administrative errors affect brackets, fixtures, or standings.

### Adoption perspective

- Not always the buyer, but critical for real operational usage.
- Needs speed, clarity, and trustworthy current state.

## Persona 4: Competitive Amateur Player

### Profile

- Registers for tournaments or amateur competitions and expects structure and fairness.

### Goals

- Register in the correct category and division.
- Know whether entry is accepted.
- Understand who they play, when they play, and how progression works.
- Trust results and standings.

### Frustrations

- Unclear eligibility rules.
- Poor visibility into brackets, schedules, or results.
- Conflicting competition updates from different channels.
- Manual organizer errors that affect fairness.

### Adoption perspective

- Usually not the first payer.
- Critical to participation and perceived trust in the competition product.
