# User Journeys

## Journey 1: Organizer creates a tournament

### Goal

Create a competition structure without rebuilding operational logic from scratch each time.

### Flow

1. The organizer creates a tournament or competition.
2. The organizer defines dates, format, categories, and sex divisions.
3. The organizer sets registration rules and administrative ownership.
4. The organizer publishes the competition for registration or internal administration.

### Desired outcome

- The organizer can launch a competition from one structured workflow instead of spreadsheets and chat threads.

## Journey 2: Player registers for competition

### Goal

Register clearly and know whether the entry is valid and accepted.

### Flow

1. The player reviews the competition and relevant category or division.
2. The player submits registration.
3. The organizer or system validates eligibility according to the rules.
4. The player receives clear status visibility.

### Desired outcome

- Registration status is explicit and category placement is understandable.

## Journey 3: Organizer validates entries and generates structure

### Goal

Move from raw registrations to a competition structure that can actually run.

### Flow

1. The organizer reviews registrations by category and sex division.
2. The organizer approves or rejects entries when needed.
3. The system generates or supports creation of the match structure.
4. The organizer reviews the resulting setup before competition play begins.

### Desired outcome

- The competition starts with a clean, trusted participant structure.

## Journey 4: Competition support staff records results

### Goal

Keep competition progression current without administrative confusion.

### Flow

1. The support user opens the relevant match or fixture.
2. The result is entered or confirmed.
3. The system updates match state and progression.
4. The organizer can immediately see what remains unresolved.

### Desired outcome

- Results stop living in disconnected channels and progression remains coherent.

## Journey 5: Organizer closes the competition

### Goal

Finish the competition with clear outcomes and usable records.

### Flow

1. The organizer verifies that all required results are complete.
2. The organizer reviews the final standings, winners, or competition outcome.
3. The competition is marked complete.
4. Participant and competition history remain available for later reference.

### Desired outcome

- The competition closes cleanly without manual reconstruction of results.

## Journey design principle

Each journey is centered on competition administration, fairness, and operational trust. If a journey requires too many manual corrections or depends on external ad hoc tracking, it should be redesigned before expanding scope.
