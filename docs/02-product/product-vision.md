# Product Vision

## Status

This document defines the product direction based on the research phase completed on 2026-04-27 and the validated strategic pivot toward competition workflow.

## Vision statement

Help padel clubs and organizers run tournaments and amateur competitions with less operational chaos and more trustworthy structure.

## Expanded vision

The product should begin as a competition workflow platform for padel, not as a generic club management suite and not as a social match coordination tool.

The first product promise is narrow:

- reduce manual effort around tournament setup, registration, category administration, and result handling,
- provide clear operational workflows for competitions segmented by category and sex,
- improve trust in competition progression, standings, and participant eligibility,
- create a model that can later extend to other sports and club competition contexts.

## Strategic position

The product is intentionally not positioned as:

- a full club ERP,
- a reservation-first booking platform,
- a player analytics product,
- a generic messaging tool.

Instead, it is positioned as a workflow product for competition administration.

## Why this vision

The original concept mixed too many products. The clearer long-term strategy is to start with a stronger abstraction:

- competition setup,
- participant eligibility,
- category and sex segmentation,
- brackets or match schedules,
- results,
- progression,
- and standings.

This is a more transferable product core than recurring social-session coordination because it can expand later into other sports and clubs without being tied to informal padel-specific social workflows.

## Primary value proposition

For clubs and organizers:

- less spreadsheet and chat dependency,
- cleaner tournament setup and execution,
- clearer control of categories, participant segmentation, and competition flow,
- better visibility into registrations, fixtures, pending results, and unresolved administrative actions.

For players:

- clearer registration and eligibility,
- better visibility into tournament structure and match progression,
- more trustworthy results and standings.

## Product principle

If the product vision starts expanding into reservations, payments, CRM, coaching, or general club operations before the competition wedge is validated, the scope is too broad again.
