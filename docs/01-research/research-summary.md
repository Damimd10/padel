# Research Summary

## Snapshot

As of 2026-04-27, the proposed padel web app should be treated as a discovery problem, not as an approved solution.

## Main conclusion

The original concept was too broad to move safely into product definition. It mixed club operations, competition management, player statistics, and community coordination without evidence that one integrated platform was the right answer.

After clarifying the intended direction, the strongest wedge is competition workflow:

- tournaments,
- amateur competitions,
- categories,
- sex-based segmentation,
- registrations,
- results,
- and progression.

## What appears true so far

- There are real workflow problems around tournament setup, participant administration, and result handling.
- The market is active and already served by multiple categories of tools.
- Existing solutions are not absent; they are often broad, fragmented, or only partially aligned with padel-specific competition workflows.
- The first buyer is more likely to be a club, organizer, or competition operator than a casual player.

## What should be challenged

- The all-in-one vision should remain unproven.
- Rankings and statistics should be treated as trust-sensitive, not as easy differentiators.
- Replacing an incumbent system may still be much harder than delivering focused competition value first.
- The product should not drift back into generic club-management scope before the competition wedge is validated.

## Most plausible opportunity direction

- A competition workflow product for amateur padel clubs and organizers.

This includes:

- category and division administration,
- participant registration and approval,
- match structure and progression,
- result handling,
- and standings visibility.

## Main risks

- Over-scoping before validation.
- Choosing competition depth that is too complex for the first version.
- Underestimating trust requirements for results and progression.
- Building a product that sounds strategic but is not painful enough to buy.

## Recommended next step

Before writing application code, continue translating this direction into:

- product scope,
- requirements,
- business rules,
- use cases,
- architecture,
- and approved backlog items.

The immediate goal is to validate one painful, repeated, commercially valuable competition workflow as the first product wedge.
