# Target Users

## Research principle

The product should not be designed for “padel players” as a single audience. Different actors buy, administer, and use software for different reasons.

## Primary user candidates

### 1. Club owner or general manager

Primary concerns:

- Court occupancy and revenue.
- Operational control.
- Staff permissions and accountability.
- Booking fairness and exception handling.
- Member retention and communication quality.

Why this segment matters:

- Most likely economic buyer for operational software.
- Feels the cost of fragmented tools directly.

### 2. Front-desk or operations staff

Primary concerns:

- Fast booking changes.
- Walk-ins, cancellations, no-shows, and court blocks.
- Member lookup and permission handling.
- Communicating changes without chasing people manually.

Why this segment matters:

- They live the day-to-day operational pain.
- They will reject any system that slows them down during peak hours.

### 3. Tournament organizer or league coordinator

Primary concerns:

- Registration flow.
- Categories and eligibility.
- Brackets, fixtures, scoring, standings, and ranking updates.
- Bulk communication with players.

Why this segment matters:

- Strong candidate for a narrower initial wedge if competition management is the real pain.

### 4. Community organizer or team captain

Primary concerns:

- Filling games.
- Finding substitutes.
- Coordinating time slots.
- Avoiding WhatsApp thread chaos.

Why this segment matters:

- Important if the opportunity is more community-led than club-led.
- Likely lower willingness to pay than clubs.

### 5. Amateur player or club member

Primary concerns:

- Easy booking.
- Clear match participation status.
- Fair access to time slots.
- Joining games with players of similar level.
- Viewing results, rankings, and schedule changes without friction.

Why this segment matters:

- Critical for adoption, but not necessarily the first payer.

## Secondary user candidates

### 6. Competitive player

Primary concerns:

- Verified match history.
- Credible ranking progression.
- Discoverable tournaments and league play.
- Better visibility into performance data.

### 7. Coach or academy operator

Primary concerns:

- Group scheduling.
- Attendance tracking.
- Player grouping by level.
- Communication with trainees and parents if junior programs exist later.

## Segmentation warnings

These segments should not be treated as interchangeable:

- Private clubs.
- Public pay-per-play venues.
- Independent tournament organizers.
- Amateur communities not tied to a single venue.
- Academies and coaching programs.

They may all use padel software, but they do not share the same workflow priorities, switching costs, or buying logic.

## Initial target hypothesis

The strongest initial buyer hypothesis is one of these:

- Club manager with recurring operational coordination pain.
- Tournament or league organizer with recurring competition administration pain.

The weakest initial buyer hypothesis is the casual player, because players already tolerate free tools unless the new product unlocks access, convenience, or status.
