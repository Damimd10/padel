# Problem Statement

## Research snapshot

This document reflects the discovery snapshot as of 2026-04-27 and the later clarification that the intended wedge is competition workflow.

## Executive framing

The original idea combined too many jobs-to-be-done:

- club operations,
- competition management,
- player development,
- community coordination.

That breadth was strategically risky because each workflow has different buyers, different success criteria, and different operational constraints.

The clarified direction is to treat competition workflow as the primary entry problem.

## Core problem hypothesis

Padel clubs and amateur organizers often manage tournaments, categories, participant eligibility, results, and progression across fragmented tools such as WhatsApp, spreadsheets, and generic club platforms. This creates avoidable admin work, poor competition visibility, low trust in results or standings, and repeated operational errors.

## Why this matters

- Organizers lose time on registration review, category management, draw setup, and result consolidation.
- Clubs struggle to run repeatable competitions without spreadsheet debt.
- Players experience confusion around category assignment, progression, and result trust.
- Competition data becomes inconsistent when registrations, brackets, and results live in separate systems.

## What should be challenged

The idea should not be accepted as correct by default. Even with this pivot, the main challenge remains scope control:

- a competition workflow product,
- a full club management platform,
- a ranking product,
- and a social coordination tool

should not be treated as the same product.

## Problem boundaries to validate

Before moving forward, we need evidence for whether these are the strongest entry pains:

- tournament and league administration,
- category and sex-based competition management,
- participant eligibility and registration control,
- result validation and progression trust.

## Initial conclusion

The most promising entry opportunity is not broad club software. It is a high-frequency, high-friction competition workflow that current tools handle badly. If that pain is not strong enough, the correct conclusion would be to narrow further, not to expand back into an all-in-one club platform.
