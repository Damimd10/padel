# Open Questions

## Product direction

- What is the first workflow we are actually trying to win?
- Is this primarily a club operations product, a competition product, or a coordination product?
- What must be in scope for discovery, and what is explicitly out of scope for now?

## Target customer

- Who is the first paying customer: club owner, operations manager, academy, organizer, or community admin?
- Which segment feels the pain most frequently and with the highest cost?
- Which segment is easiest to onboard without heavy customization?

## Market focus

- Which geography are we targeting first?
- Are we focused on mature padel markets, emerging markets, or a local niche?
- How do workflows differ by country, club model, or market maturity?

## Current behavior

- What tools are target users using today for bookings, communication, results, and rankings?
- Which parts of the workflow happen in WhatsApp, spreadsheets, incumbent club software, or manual processes?
- What do users hate enough to change, versus tolerate as “just how clubs work”?

## Reservations and scheduling

- Are reservations a system-of-record requirement or a workflow to integrate with?
- How do clubs currently handle peak-hour fairness, waitlists, guest players, and recurring slots?
- Is self-assignment actually desired by operators, or do they prefer controlled allocation?

## Competition and rankings

- What type of competition matters most: tournaments, leagues, ladders, americanos, social matches?
- What ranking model is trusted by the target segment: ladder, points, ELO-like, category-based, coach-validated, or federation-aligned?
- Who can submit results, and who confirms them?
- How are disputes resolved today?

## Communication

- Do users want native communication inside the product, or structured coordination that still delivers through WhatsApp, email, or push?
- What kinds of communication must be role-based, auditable, or automated?
- Which notifications are valuable, and which become noise?

## Business model

- What value metric would a buyer actually pay for: courts, active players, tournaments, staff seats, or premium workflows?
- Would buyers pay for a replacement platform, an add-on layer, or a premium competition module?
- Can the product prove ROI quickly enough to shorten sales cycles?

## Implementation implications for later phases

- Which data must be authoritative from day one?
- Which workflows require integrations with third-party systems?
- What privacy, consent, and data retention obligations apply in the target market?

## Mandatory next-step research

Before continuing to product scope, we should answer these with interviews and workflow mapping:

- What is the top painful weekly workflow?
- Who owns it today?
- What tools are involved?
- What fails most often?
- What would make users switch behavior?
