# Opportunities

## Opportunity filter

An opportunity is only interesting if it solves a painful workflow better than current habits and tools. “More features” is not an opportunity by itself.

## Most credible opportunity areas

### 1. Coordination layer for exceptions

Possible angle:

- Solve the ugly real-world cases: cancellations, substitutions, open slots, waitlists, and late confirmations.

Why this matters:

- This pain is frequent.
- It is poorly handled by chat threads and only partially handled by booking tools.
- It is valuable for both clubs and community organizers.

Why this is promising:

- It targets repeated operational friction instead of generic platform ambition.

### 2. Trusted amateur competition workflows

Possible angle:

- Make leagues, ladders, americanos, round robins, rankings, and result confirmation easier to run without spreadsheet debt.

Why this matters:

- Padel communities often want structured competition before they need full enterprise club management.
- Ranking and recurring play can increase retention if trust is high.

Why this is risky:

- Ranking credibility is hard.
- If result verification is weak, the product collapses socially.

### 3. Overlay instead of replacement

Possible angle:

- Integrate with incumbent systems for bookings while owning a narrower workflow such as coordination, competition, or communication.

Why this matters:

- Reduces switching resistance.
- Lets the product enter clubs where replacement would be unrealistic.

Why this is risky:

- Integration dependency may weaken product power and margins.

### 4. Cross-club identity and level portability

Possible angle:

- Help players and organizers interpret level and participation history across venues.

Why this matters:

- Current ranking and level systems are fragmented.
- Travelling players and mixed-club communities feel this friction directly.

Why this is risky:

- Standardizing level across clubs is politically and operationally difficult.

### 5. Communication with operational structure

Possible angle:

- Keep the speed of messaging while adding targeted notifications, traceability, status visibility, and cleaner coordination flows.

Why this matters:

- WhatsApp remains the behavioral default, but it is operationally weak.

Why this is risky:

- Users may not adopt a new communication tool unless it clearly beats existing habits.

## Weak opportunities to avoid early

- A generic stats dashboard without trusted input data.
- A full all-in-one club ERP from day one.
- A player-only app that depends on organic network effects without club or organizer pull.
- Building reservations, tournaments, rankings, communication, payments, and CRM all at once.

## Early opportunity hypotheses worth testing

- Clubs need help filling abandoned slots and managing replacements faster.
- Organizers need a lighter-weight competition workflow than existing club systems provide.
- Players will adopt a new workflow if it gives access to better-balanced matches and less coordination friction.
- Clubs may buy an operational overlay sooner than they buy a platform replacement.

## Recommendation for next discovery step

Do not choose an opportunity based on product elegance. Choose based on:

- Frequency of the problem.
- Cost of the current workaround.
- Willingness to change behavior.
- Ease of entering the workflow without replacing too much.
